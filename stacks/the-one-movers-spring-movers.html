What Most People Get Wrong About Moving & Storage

Most folks think they can handle moving without professional help because "how hard could boxing stuff really be?" Here's the reality: DIY moves in Spring typically cost $250-$500 more than planned once you factor in damaged furniture, wasted packing materials, and multiple truck rental days.

After helping over 340 families move in the Spring and Woodlands area since 2019, we've seen the same mistakes repeatedly. By the time they call us to fix broken furniture legs or retrieve items from botched storage jobs, they've spent more money and triple the time.

Why Spring's Climate Makes Professional Moving Worth It

In our experience working Spring moves year-round, humidity is your enemy. Between May and September, temperatures hit 95-102°F with 70-85% humidity. We've watched DIY movers leave cardboard boxes in driveways along Louetta Road or near Old Town Spring for "just 20 minutes" while making multiple trips. Those boxes absorb moisture, bottoms fall out, and ceramic dishes shatter on concrete.

At The One Movers, we load entire homes in one continuous process. Our trucks stay climate-controlled, and we've never had a moisture-damaged box in four years of operation. We also know which Spring apartment complexes (looking at you, Cypresswood Place and Legends at Old Town) require elevator reservations 72 hours ahead and which ones charge $150-$300 fines for lobby damage.

Storage Reality in Spring, TX

Here's something most people don't realize: Spring sits in a flood zone (check FEMA maps for areas near Spring Creek and Cypress Creek). Standard storage units at Public Storage or CubeSmart on Louetta Road run $85-$165 monthly for 10x10 units, but ground-level units flood during heavy rain events like we saw in April 2024 (13 inches in 6 hours).

At us, we only recommend second-floor climate-controlled units for Spring residents, which cost $120-$190 monthly but protect against the flooding we see 2-3 times yearly. We've helped families recover from flooded storage—waterlogged couches ($800-$2,200 loss), ruined mattresses ($400-$1,800), and mildewed boxes of family photos (irreplaceable).
